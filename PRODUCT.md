# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Three overlapping audiences, each with a distinct conversion goal:

1. **Partners & funders** — Government bodies, NGOs, institutional donors evaluating whether to back or co-run the outreach. They need credibility signals: a clear scope, honest about what's confirmed vs. planned, and a contact path.
2. **Volunteers & healthcare professionals** — Clinicians, community health workers, and organizers who could contribute time or skill to the pilot. They need to understand the model and know how to get involved.
3. **Community members** — Women and families in Kwara State who should know when, where, and why to show up. They need clarity and approachability, not institutional distance.

All three arrive at the same site; each needs a clear onward path.

## Product Purpose

NovaWell Community Health Initiative is preparing its first rural outreach: free screenings, reproductive health education, and referral guidance brought directly to women in Kwara State who face barriers to preventive care. The site exists to attract the partners, volunteers, and community awareness the pilot needs to launch — and to demonstrate that NovaWell is a credible, organized, honest operator, not just a promise.

Success = a partner reaches out, a healthcare worker signs up, or a community member knows the outreach is coming to their area.

## Brand Personality

**Grounded. Credible. Purposeful.**

The voice is that of a serious health organization that earned its confidence through careful preparation, not hype. It is warm toward the communities it serves and rigorous with partners. It never overpromises. The emotional register is quiet urgency — the work matters, the timeline is real, and every sentence earns its place.

## Anti-references

- **Generic charity site** — Stock-photo NGO aesthetic, forgettable layout, language that could describe any organization anywhere. NovaWell has a specific geography, a specific pilot, specific constraints. The site should feel like that.
- **Corporate health brand** — Cold, clinical, pharma-adjacent. NovaWell is not a hospital system or a multinational. The design should not feel like it belongs in a corporate annual report.

## Design Principles

1. **Evidence over aspiration** — Show what is confirmed; be honest about what is still being finalized. The pilot's narrow, focused scope is a strength. Don't pad it with vague ambitions.
2. **Institutional credibility, human scale** — Professional enough to earn the trust of government partners and funders. Human enough that a woman in Ilorin feels the program is for her.
3. **Rooted in place** — Visual and editorial choices should feel tied to Nigeria, to Kwara, to the specific communities served — not designed for a Western donor audience in London or New York.
4. **Every audience has a path** — Partners, volunteers, and community members each need a visible next action. The site converts three different intents without fragmenting into three separate sites.
5. **Earned confidence** — The program is in its pilot phase. The design communicates seriousness and competence without overclaiming scale or impact that hasn't happened yet.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

- Target: WCAG AA
- Keyboard navigation and screen reader support throughout
- Strong contrast ratios (≥4.5:1 body text, ≥3:1 large/bold text)
- Reduced motion respected — all animations have `prefers-reduced-motion` alternatives
- Performance: fast initial load for users on mobile connections
